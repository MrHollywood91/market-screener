WAMCO (Western Asset Management) — SEC Cherry-Picking Enforcement Action

Western Asset Management Company (WAMCO), a subsidiary of Franklin Resources (BEN), agreed to a $100 million settlement with the SEC over allegations of systematic "cherry-picking" — the practice of allocating winning trades to favored accounts (including proprietary or preferred client accounts) while assigning losing trades to other clients. This is one of the largest cherry-picking settlements in SEC history and directly impacts Franklin Resources (BEN) shareholders, as WAMCO is a material revenue contributor. The case covered conduct spanning multiple years and involved thousands of trade allocations across fixed income portfolios. BEN shares remain under pressure given concurrent outflows from active management and this regulatory overhang. Current status: settlement terms finalized, with WAMCO required to implement comprehensive trade allocation compliance reforms. Financial exposure: $100M fine plus remediation costs and potential client redemptions estimated by analysts at $2-5B in AUM at risk.
