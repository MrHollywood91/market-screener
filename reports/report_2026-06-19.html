1. WAMCO (Western Asset Management) — SEC Cherry-Picking Settlement | $100M Fine

Western Asset Management Company (WAMCO), a subsidiary of Franklin Templeton (BEN), has agreed to a $100 million settlement with the SEC over allegations of systematic cherry-picking — the illegal practice of selectively allocating profitable trades to favored accounts (typically the manager's own or preferred clients) while routing losing trades to disadvantaged client accounts. The SEC's case alleged the misconduct spanned multiple years and impacted thousands of retail and institutional client accounts. The $100M fine represents one of the larger investment adviser enforcement actions in recent memory and sets a significant precedent for how the current administration's SEC approaches allocation fraud. *Current status:* Settlement finalized; no admission of wrongdoing per standard SEC resolution terms. *Financial exposure:* Beyond the $100M penalty, Franklin Templeton (BEN) faces reputational damage and potential client redemptions from WAMCO-managed mandates, which could pressure AUM figures in upcoming quarterly reports. Watch BEN for analyst estimate revisions.

2. Elon Musk / X Corp — SEC Twitter Disclosure Settlement | $1.5M Fine

Elon Musk reached a settlement with the SEC over his failure to timely disclose his accumulation of a greater-than-5% stake in Twitter (now X Corp) in early 2022, a violation of Section 13(d) of the Securities Exchange Act. The delayed disclosure — filed approximately 11 days late — allowed Musk to continue purchasing shares at artificially lower prices before the market learned of his position, resulting in an estimated $150M+ in underpayment relative to what he would have paid had disclosure been timely. The $1.5M fine has been widely criticized as disproportionately small relative to the financial benefit gained. *Current status:* Settlement agreed; Musk neither admitted nor denied wrongdoing. *Financial exposure:* Minimal direct financial impact on Musk's net worth, but the settlement creates legal precedent and raises governance questions for Tesla (TSLA) investors who monitor Musk's regulatory relationships. The SEC's acceptance of a $1.5M settlement under the current administration is being scrutinized by securities law practitioners as a signal of reduced enforcement appetite for high-profile defendants.
