Section 3 — Major Public Company Lawsuits & Regulatory Actions

Western Asset Management (WAMCO) — SEC Cherry-Picking & DOJ Settlement | $100M Penalty

Western Asset Management Co., the fixed-income arm of Franklin Templeton (BEN), reached a $100 million civil penalty settlement with the SEC resolving long-standing allegations of "cherry-picking" — the illegal practice of allocating winning trades to favored accounts while assigning losing trades to disadvantaged clients. The DOJ simultaneously closed its parallel criminal probe, providing WAMCO and its parent Franklin Templeton with meaningful legal closure on the matter. The settlement is among the largest in recent memory for trade allocation fraud, and while Franklin Templeton (BEN) absorbed the financial hit, the reputational damage to WAMCO's institutional fixed-income franchise — which manages hundreds of billions in pension, endowment, and sovereign wealth mandates — is likely more costly long-term. The case establishes an important enforcement benchmark under the current SEC administration for how cherry-picking violations will be adjudicated and priced, per Skadden's recent client guidance.

Elon Musk / X (formerly Twitter) — SEC Disclosure Settlement | Timeline & Terms

The Securities and Exchange Commission formally settled its long-running enforcement action against Elon Musk related to his 2022 acquisition of Twitter, specifically his alleged failure to timely disclose crossing the 5% beneficial ownership threshold — a violation of Section 13(d) of the Securities Exchange Act that cost other investors the opportunity to sell shares at a premium before Musk's purchases became public. The settlement terms, reported by The New York Times, represent a resolution to one of the highest-profile SEC enforcement targets of the era. The case carries implications beyond Musk personally: it reaffirms that Schedule 13D/13G disclosure timelines remain an active SEC enforcement priority even under the current administration, which has generally been perceived as more business-friendly. For Tesla (TSLA) shareholders, legal resolution removes an overhang, though Musk's governance bandwidth remains a persistent investor concern.

SEC vs. Public Company Resolutions — Emerging Framework Under Current Administration

Per guidance published by Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher & Flom LLP, the SEC's recent settlement patterns with public companies are establishing a new precedent framework for how corporations resolve enforcement actions in the current regulatory environment. The guidance suggests the current SEC is favoring negotiated settlements with defined cooperation credits over prolonged litigation, and is placing particular emphasis on disgorgement of ill-gotten gains plus meaningful civil penalties rather than structural remedies. For compliance professionals and investor relations teams at public companies, the takeaway is that self-reporting and early cooperation meaningfully reduces financial exposure. This framework will be closely watched by companies currently under informal SEC inquiry, particularly in sectors facing heightened regulatory scrutiny including financial services, cryptocurrency, and pharmaceutical pricing.
